Competition overview

The Battle of Beersbridge is a same-sex pairs competition consisting of four events and six scoring opportunities.

The competition has been designed to test a broad range of CrossFit skills, including endurance, strength, weightlifting, gymnastics and teamwork. Teams will compete across a variety of workout formats, with reseeding after each event based on the current leaderboard.

Format

  • Teams of two
  • Same-sex pairs
  • RX and Intermediate divisions
  • Six teams per division
  • Four events
  • Six scoring opportunities
  • Two heats of three teams
  • Heats reseeded after every event
  • Approximately six-hour competition
  • £2,750 prize fund (entry dependent)

General competition rules

The following rules apply to all athletes throughout the competition unless otherwise stated within an individual event brief.

Athlete conduct

All athletes are expected to demonstrate professionalism, respect and good sportsmanship throughout the day. Unsportsmanlike behaviour towards judges, volunteers, spectators or fellow competitors may result in penalties or disqualification at the discretion of the Competition Director.

Judges

Each team will be assigned a judge for every event. Judges are responsible for:

  • Counting repetitions
  • Applying movement standards
  • Recording scores
  • Communicating no-repetitions

The judge's decision is final.

Appeals

There will be no formal appeals process during the competition. If a scoring discrepancy is identified, the team captain should speak to the Head Judge immediately after the event has concluded and before the next heat begins. Judgement calls and no-repetition decisions cannot be appealed.

Teamwork

Athletes may encourage and communicate with their teammate throughout every event unless specifically stated otherwise. Athletes may not physically assist their teammate unless the workout explicitly allows it. Examples of prohibited assistance include:

  • Assisting with lifts
  • Supporting bodyweight
  • Pushing, pulling or carrying a teammate
  • Touching equipment being used by the other athlete

Equipment

Only the prescribed competition equipment may be used. Athletes may not modify, move or substitute equipment unless instructed by a judge. Equipment must remain within the assigned competition lane unless directed otherwise.

Chalk

Loose chalk and liquid chalk are permitted. Athletes may chalk their hands during the workout provided it does not interfere with another team or delay the flow of competition.

Personal equipment

Athletes may use:

  • Weightlifting belts
  • Wrist wraps
  • Knee sleeves
  • Knee wraps
  • Hand grips
  • Gymnastics tape
  • Finger tape
  • Wrist tape

The use of lifting straps is not permitted. Headphones or personal audio devices are not permitted while competing.

Scoring

Scores will be recorded immediately following each event. It is the responsibility of both athletes to verify their score before leaving the competition floor. Once a score has been signed and submitted it is considered final.

Tie breaks

If two teams finish with the same score in an event, any published tie-break score will determine the finishing order. Where no tie-break is available, teams will receive the same placing for that event.

Time caps

Each event has a published time cap. If a team does not complete the workout within the time cap, their score will be recorded according to the workout's scoring system. Any unfinished repetitions will be recorded as instructed within the event brief.

No reps

A judge may issue a no-repetition for any movement that does not meet the published movement standard. Athletes are responsible for correcting the fault before continuing. Only successful repetitions count towards the team's score.

Safety

Athlete safety will always take priority over competition. The Competition Director and Head Judge reserve the right to stop any athlete they believe is unable to continue safely. Medical personnel will be available throughout the day.

Warm-up areas

Athletes are welcome to use the designated warm-up area between events. Please be respectful of other athletes and return equipment after use. Competition equipment may not be removed from the field of play.

Athlete call

Athletes should report to the designated marshalling area at least 10 minutes before their scheduled heat. Failure to report on time may result in the team missing their heat.

Spectators

Spectators are welcome throughout the day. Only competing athletes, judges and authorised volunteers are permitted inside the competition field of play.

Photography & media

Photographs and video will be taken throughout the competition. By participating, athletes consent to the use of this content by CrossFit TBB for promotional and marketing purposes.

Competition formats

Throughout the competition, movements will use one of the following work-sharing formats.

Synchro

Both athletes perform every repetition together. The prescribed synchro point for each movement is detailed within the event standards. If the synchro requirement is not met, the repetition will not count.

Shared as desired

The prescribed work may be divided between teammates in any way unless otherwise stated. Athletes may change over at any time.

Split equally

The prescribed work must be divided equally between both athletes. Each athlete must complete exactly half of the total prescribed work.

Assigned

Each athlete has a designated movement or workload. Assignments are detailed within the event brief and must be completed as prescribed.

Event 01

Running Grace

Overview

Event One begins the Battle of Beersbridge with a fast-paced twist on the classic CrossFit benchmark workout, Grace. Teams will compete head-to-head within their assigned heat, racing against both the clock and the team beside them. Following Event One, all teams will be reseeded based on their performance for the remainder of the competition.

The event consists of a six-minute race to complete the workout, followed immediately by a one-minute window to establish a max distance double broad jump.

Workout

On a 7:00 rolling clock...
5 rounds for time:
6 dumbbell hang clean to overhead
3 shuttle runs
At 6:00:
Max distance double broad jump

Time cap: 7:00

Scoring

Score A

Time to complete all five rounds. If the workout is not completed before 6:00, the score will be the total rounds and repetitions completed.

Tie break: Time to complete round 1.

Score B

Maximum single distance achieved during the double broad jump.

Tie break: Score A.

Event flow

Each team will be assigned a competition lane. At the sound of 3, 2, 1, go, teams begin with six synchronised dumbbell hang clean to overhead repetitions.

Once all six repetitions have been completed, both athletes complete three shuttle runs. Each shuttle consists of 10 metres out and 10 metres back, completed three times before returning to the dumbbells.

Teams continue in this format until:

  • All five rounds are completed.
  • The clock reaches 6:00.

At exactly 6:00, all remaining work stops and teams immediately move to the double broad jump station. Only one athlete may attempt the jump at a time. Athletes may complete as many attempts as they wish during the final minute.

Every jump begins with both feet behind the start line. The landing must be controlled before the distance is measured. Distance will be measured from the start line to the heel closest to the start line.

Workout standards

Dumbbell hang clean to overhead

Standard

  • Teams complete all repetitions synchronously.
  • The dumbbells begin below the hips.
  • The movement may be performed as a clean and jerk, clean and push press, clean and push jerk or clean and split jerk.
  • Both dumbbells must pass through the front rack before moving overhead.
  • Athletes do not need to move at the same speed.
  • At some point during the repetition, both athletes must be locked out overhead together before either athlete lowers their dumbbells.
  • The repetition is complete when hips and knees are fully extended, elbows are fully locked out, and dumbbells are under control overhead.

No rep

  • Failure to reach full overhead lockout.
  • Failure to reach full hip or knee extension.
  • Either athlete lowers the dumbbells before both athletes have achieved the synchronisation point.
  • Failure to establish control overhead before lowering the dumbbells.

Shuttle run

Standard

  • Teams complete every shuttle together.
  • Each shuttle consists of 10 metres to the turnaround line and 10 metres back to the start line.
  • Three complete shuttles must be completed each round.
  • Athletes must remain connected by the prescribed tether throughout the run.
  • At each turnaround, both athletes must touch the ground with one hand behind the line before changing direction.
  • The repetition is complete when both athletes have completed all three prescribed shuttles.

No rep

  • Failure for both athletes to touch the ground behind the turnaround line.
  • Failure for both athletes to complete all three prescribed shuttles.
  • Breaking or removing the tether during the run.
  • Missing any section of the prescribed running course.

Double broad jump

Standard

  • Begins immediately at the 6:00 mark.
  • Only one athlete may attempt a jump at a time.
  • Athletes may complete as many attempts as time allows.
  • Every attempt begins with both feet behind the start line.
  • Both feet must leave the floor simultaneously.
  • The landing must be controlled before the judge measures the distance.
  • Distance will be measured from the start line to the heel closest to the take-off line.

No rep

  • Feet begin on or over the start line.
  • Athlete fails to demonstrate control on landing.
  • Any body part behind the heels contacts the floor before the measurement is taken.
  • Both athletes attempt a jump at the same time.
Event 02

Chip Away

Overview

Event Two is a fully synchronised long chipper designed to test teamwork and consistency under fatigue. Teams must work through three movements for time, completing every repetition in the prescribed order. Every repetition throughout the workout is performed synchronously, requiring both athletes to work together from start to finish.

Workout

For time:
45 wall balls
45 deadlifts
15 burpee box jump overs
30 wall balls
30 deadlifts
15 burpee box jump overs
15 wall balls
15 deadlifts
15 burpee box jump overs

Time cap: 25:00

Scoring

Time to complete the workout. If the workout is not completed within the time cap, the score will be the total repetitions completed.

Tie break: Time to complete 45 wall balls, deadlifts and 15 burpees.

Event flow

Teams begin at the wall ball station. Once all 45 wall balls have been completed, teams move directly to the deadlift station. After completing all 45 deadlifts, teams move immediately to the burpee box jump overs. This sequence is repeated until all prescribed work has been completed.

Athletes are responsible for changing the weight on their own barbell before beginning each new deadlift section. There is no rest between movements.

  • Wall ball target — 10/9ft male/female
  • Deadlift barbell — 100-120-140/65-80-95kg RX, 80-100-120/50-65-80kg Inters
  • Competition plates
  • Box — 30/24" RX, 24/20" Inters

Workout standards

Wall ball

Standard

  • Every repetition is completed synchronously.
  • Both athletes must squat below parallel.
  • Both athletes must reach the bottom of the squat together.
  • The wall balls do not have to strike the target at the same time.
  • Each wall ball must clearly contact the prescribed target.
  • The repetition is complete once both wall balls have contacted the prescribed target.
  • In the RX division, two different wall ball weights will be used. Athletes may swap wall balls at any time.

No rep

  • Failure to squat below parallel.
  • Failure for either wall ball to contact the prescribed target.
  • Either athlete leaves the bottom of the squat before their teammate reaches the synchro position.
  • Either athlete drops their wall ball before the repetition is complete.

Deadlift

Standard

  • Every repetition is completed synchronously.
  • Barbells begin on the floor.
  • Athletes lift the barbell from the floor to full extension.
  • Athletes do not have to move at the same speed.
  • Both athletes must be standing at full lockout together before either athlete begins lowering the barbell.
  • The repetition is complete once both barbells have returned to the floor.
  • Athletes are responsible for changing the prescribed weight before the next deadlift section.

No rep

  • Failure to reach full hip or knee extension.
  • Either athlete begins lowering the barbell before both athletes have reached full lockout together.
  • Bouncing the barbell to gain an advantage.

Burpee box jump over

Standard

  • Every repetition is completed synchronously.
  • Athletes may face either direction.
  • Chest and thighs must make contact with the floor.
  • Athletes do not have to move at the same speed.
  • Both athletes must be on the floor together before either athlete begins standing.
  • Athletes must jump onto the box with two feet. Step-ups and step-overs are not permitted.
  • Athletes must jump over the box.
  • Both athletes must be standing on top of the box together before either athlete leaves the box.
  • The repetition is complete once both athletes have landed on the opposite side of the box.

No rep

  • Failure for chest and thighs to touch the floor.
  • Either athlete leaves the floor before both athletes have reached the synchro position.
  • Step-up or step-over used instead of jumping.
  • Either athlete leaves the top of the box before both athletes have reached the synchro position.
  • Failure to jump over the box.
Event 03

Push Pull

Overview

Event Three is a two-part event designed to test upper body muscular endurance and high-skill gymnastics under fatigue. The first scored section combines skipping, bench press and a high-skill gymnastics movement. Following a four-minute rest, athletes move into a sprint finish on the Echo Bike before accumulating as many bar muscle-ups as possible within the remaining time.

Event Three contains two scoring opportunities.

Total rounds and repetitions completed during Part A.

Event flow

Part A

Double unders and bench press may be divided between teammates in any way. The final movement is completed you go, I go.

  • RX: 2 peg board ascents (1 ascent each)
  • Inters: 2 rope climbs (1 climb each)

Teams continue completing rounds until the eight-minute time cap expires.

Rest

Teams have four minutes to recover and move to the Echo Bike.

Part B

Teams must first complete the prescribed calories on the Echo Bike. Calories may be divided between teammates in any way. Once all calories have been completed, teams immediately begin accumulating bar muscle-ups, divided between teammates in any way until time expires.

Workout standards

Double unders

Standard

  • Repetitions may be divided between teammates in any way.
  • The rope must pass under the feet twice for every jump.
  • The rope must continue moving forward throughout the repetition.

No rep

  • Rope passes under the feet only once.
  • Missed jump.

Bench press

Standard

  • Repetitions may be divided between teammates in any way.
  • Each team has one dedicated bench press station.
  • Every repetition begins with the barbell at full elbow lockout.
  • The barbell must make clear contact with the athlete's chest.
  • The repetition is complete when the athlete returns to full elbow lockout.

No rep

  • Failure to begin from full lockout.
  • Failure for the barbell to touch the chest.
  • Failure to reach full elbow lockout.

Peg board (RX)

Standard

  • Completed you go, I go. Each athlete completes one ascent per round.
  • Both pegs must begin below the taped start line.
  • During the ascent, both pegs must be placed into holes above the designated finish line.
  • Male athletes: above the black line. Female athletes: above the red line.
  • The repetition is complete once both pegs have returned below the taped start line.

No rep

  • Failure to place both pegs above the designated finish line.
  • Failure to return both pegs below the taped start line before beginning the next ascent.

Rope climb (Inters)

Standard

  • Completed you go, I go. Each athlete completes one climb per round.
  • The repetition begins with both feet on the floor.
  • The athlete must touch the top beam before descending.
  • The repetition is complete once both feet have returned to the floor.

No rep

  • Failure to touch the top beam.
  • Failure to return both feet to the floor before the next athlete begins.

Echo Bike

Standard

  • Calories may be divided between teammates in any way.
  • Teams must complete all prescribed calories before beginning the bar muscle-ups.
  • Athletes may change over at any time.

No rep

  • Beginning bar muscle-ups before all prescribed calories have been completed.

Bar muscle-up

Standard

  • Repetitions may be divided between teammates in any way.
  • Athletes may perform consecutive repetitions or alternate at any time.
  • The movement begins from a hang beneath the bar.
  • Kipping and uprise techniques are permitted. Glide kips are not permitted.
  • The repetition is complete once the athlete reaches full support above the bar with elbows fully locked out.

No rep

  • Failure to reach full lockout above the bar.
  • Glide kip used.
  • Loss of control before the repetition is complete.
Event 04

The Finale

Overview

Event Four is the final test of The Battle of Beersbridge. This event combines weightlifting and inverted gymnastics under fatigue. Throughout the workout, one athlete will complete the sandbag while their partner completes the squat snatches. Once both athletes have completed their assigned movement, they will complete the prescribed gymnastics movement before beginning the next round.

Workout

For time...
4 rounds:
4 sandbag to shoulder
6 squat snatches
80ft handstand walk (RX) || 8 wall walks (Inters)
Directly into...
4 rounds:
4 sandbag to shoulder
6 squat snatches
8 strict deficit handstand push ups (RX) || 8 handstand push ups (Inters)

Time cap: 18:00

Scoring

Time to complete the workout. If the workout is not completed within the time cap, the score will be the total repetitions completed.

Tie break: Time to complete round 1.

Event flow

At the start of every round:

  • One athlete completes all four sandbag to shoulder repetitions.
  • Their teammate completes all six squat snatches.
  • These can be worked through at the same time.

Athletes must switch weightlifting implements every round, meaning each athlete completes four rounds on the sandbag and four rounds on the squat snatches.

Once both athletes have completed their assigned movement, they move together to the prescribed gymnastics movement. Gymnastic reps must be split equally.

Workout standards

Sandbag to shoulder

Standard

  • One athlete completes all four repetitions while their partner completes the squat snatches.
  • The sandbag begins on the floor and may be lifted to either shoulder.
  • The repetition is complete when the athlete is standing at full extension, the sandbag is under control on the shoulder, and the hand opposite the supporting shoulder is visibly away from the body.
  • The sandbag may be dropped once the repetition is complete.

No rep

  • Failure to reach full extension.
  • Failure to establish control on the shoulder.
  • Opposite hand remains in contact with the sandbag or body at completion.

Squat snatch

Standard

  • One athlete completes all six repetitions while their partner completes the sandbag.
  • The barbell must move from the floor to overhead in one continuous movement.
  • The athlete must pass through a squat, with the hip crease clearly below the top of the knee before standing.
  • The repetition is complete when the athlete reaches full hip, knee and elbow extension with the barbell overhead.
  • Touch-and-go repetitions are permitted.

No rep

  • Failure to pass below parallel.
  • Failure to reach full overhead lockout.
  • Loss of control before the repetition is complete.

Handstand walk (RX)

Standard

  • Teams complete 80ft each round. Each athlete must complete 40ft each round.
  • The distance must be completed in minimum 10ft increments, in any combination of 10ft lengths or greater.

No rep

  • Failure to complete a minimum 10ft before changing athletes.
  • Failure to complete the prescribed 40ft per athlete.
  • Failure to complete the full 80ft as a team.

Wall walk (Inters)

Standard

  • Teams complete 8 repetitions each round. Each athlete must complete 4 repetitions.
  • Each repetition begins with the athlete lying prone on the floor with the feet touching the wall.
  • Hands must be touching the start line before beginning the repetition, and must remain there until both feet are in contact with the wall.
  • The athlete walks the hands to the target line. Hands must return to the start line before the feet leave the wall.
  • The repetition is complete once both feet and the chest have returned to the floor.

No rep

  • Hands leave the start line before both feet are on the wall.
  • Failure to reach the target line.
  • Hands do not return to the start line before the feet leave the wall.
  • Failure to return both feet and chest to the floor.
  • One athlete completes more than four repetitions.

Strict deficit handstand push up (RX)

Standard

  • Teams complete 8 repetitions each round. Each athlete must complete 4 repetitions.
  • Every repetition begins from a fully locked out handstand position. Only the athlete's heels may be in contact with the wall.
  • Hands must remain on the marked lines throughout the repetition.
  • The head must make clear contact with the prescribed target.
  • The repetition is complete once the athlete returns to full elbow lockout. Strict repetitions only.

No rep

  • Use of a kip.
  • Failure to begin from full lockout.
  • Failure to touch the prescribed target.
  • Failure to reach full elbow lockout.
  • Any part of the body other than the heels contacts the wall.
  • Hands leave the marked lines.
  • One athlete completes more than four repetitions.

Handstand push up (Inters)

Standard

  • Teams complete 8 repetitions each round. Each athlete must complete 4 repetitions.
  • Every repetition begins from a fully locked out handstand position. Only the athlete's heels may be in contact with the wall.
  • Hands must remain on the marked lines throughout the repetition.
  • The head must make clear contact with the prescribed target.
  • The repetition is complete once the athlete returns to full elbow lockout. Strict and kipping repetitions are permitted.

No rep

  • Failure to begin from full lockout.
  • Failure to touch the prescribed target.
  • Failure to reach full elbow lockout.
  • Any part of the body other than the heels contacts the wall.
  • Hands leave the marked lines.
  • One athlete completes more than four repetitions.
